University of Rochester has many health services available to students. Use this page to find information about helpful resources and how to use them.
Physical Health
University Health Services (UHS) assigns each student a Primary Care Provider (PCP). Your PCP can help you with any health needs. To schedule an appointment, call (585) 275-2662 or walk in to UHS, say you would like to schedule an appointment with your PCP, they will ask your name and birthday to confirm identity, then they find a time for you to come in for an appointment that works with your schedule. You may also have to provide a reason for the visit, which can be as vague or specific as you want. Afterward, you can contact your PCP via UHS Connect, the online messaging and forms system.

Condoms are available for free, or you can request to have them discreetly delivered in an envelope in your CMC Box. Birth control pills and other a variety of other contraceptives are available at UHS as well.
Mental Health
The University Counseling Center (UCC) provides initial assessments, individualized treatment plans, and support to put such a plan into action. Services available include individual and couples psychotherapy, therapist assisted online therapy (TAO), TAO self-help, group therapy, and light therapy for Seasonal Affective Disorder. To make an appointment, the process is the same as for UHS (see above), but the phone number is (585) 275-3113. A UCC Professional is always on call at that number if you would like to speak to someone immediately.
Concern About Yourself or Others
If you are concerned about the wellness of another student or yourself, you can submit a concern referral to the CARE Network and see the resources available on their website. The CARE Team can help with academic, emotional, financial, physical, social, and spiritual concerns. After submitting the form, the student will be contacted confidentially and consult with the CARE Team to best address their needs to help them thrive at University of Rochester.
Disability Services
The Office of Disability Resources provides students with academic and residential accommodations. You can visit their office to register as a new intake which gives you access to their online portal.
